General tips: 

Make calls after 10 am and before 8 pm.

For your initial call, try to make sure you get through all the questions in the call script if you can.

Expect some challenges. If you are unsure how to deal with a situation, you can always contact us.

Don’t offer advice or expertise, or make promises.

End the call with a laugh!

If you’re looking for some conversation starters to use during your ongoing calls, you can check out some of the questions below. They are adapted from [Storycorp Connect](https://storycorpsorg-staging.s3.amazonaws.com/uploads/great-questions.pdf).

**General questions**

Can you tell me about the person who has been kindest to you in your life?
Can you tell me about one of your happiest memories?
Can you tell me about someone who has had a big influence on your life? What lessons did that person teach you?
What do you feel most grateful for in your life?
What are some of the most important lessons you’ve learned in life?
What are you proudest of in your life?
How has your life been different than what you’d imagined?
How would you like to be remembered?
Do you have any regrets?

**Coronavirus-related questions**

How has living through this experience made you feel?
Are you afraid? What frightens you?
What has given you comfort and hope during this time?
What has been the most challenging part of this experience?
What is the toughest part of your days right now? The best?
What’s the biggest way your life has changed because of the pandemic?
Is there someone you can’t see right now who you really wish you could?
How is this experience different from other historical events you’ve lived through?
What memory of this time do you think will stay with you?
Has this experience changed you? If so, how?
What have you learned from this experience?
Who do you most worry about in your family or circle of friends right now?
